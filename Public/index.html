How does this work?

Simply. Everyday at 3:00 pm (EST), the NeighborLine is open. Callers will be placed on the line with another caller for a nice, neighborly chat without ever having to share their telephone numbers.

Neighborline is for striking up a conversation with a neighbor that might otherwise occur in line at the grocery store or while riding the bus. It’s a way to check on and in with your fellow community members cooped up at home.




Who is this for?

Everyone. Neighnorline is an experience designed to fill a communication void for localized communities in isolation who might not be a part of highly-networked circles online, like the elderly or those without access to the internet. But really, NeighborLine is for anyone who has a phone––cell-phone, smartphone, landline, or otherwise.

Right now, NeighborLine has the capacity to serve New Yorkers. We hope NeighborLine can be a model for other areas around the country and the world. We’re providing all the documentation and resources for NeighborLine to inspire others to replicate, reimagine, and rediscover the magic of a phone call.




Community Guidelines

Be a good neighbor. Be kind, courteous, and treat others the way you would want to be treated. Remember, these are stressful days for everyone.

1. There is no room for hatred or intolerance on NeighborLine.
2. This project ensures you don’t have to share your phone number. In your conversations, take precautions to safeguard your private and personal information.
3. NeighborLine is not a crisis line. For help and critical support, please take a look at the resources provided below.

 We urge you to settle issues in a neighborly way.

Why we made this

In the new reality of Covid-19, there’s been an internet renaissance. Confined to apartments here in New York City, and in homes around the world, people have found creativity, humor, joy, and connection in video chats and online platforms. Social media has never been quite this, well, social. And while so many are enjoying virtual dance parties, clinking glasses over the screen, and streaming live performances from their living rooms, we wondered: where does this leave our elderly and offline neighbors? Neighborline aims to bridge low-fi communications with a high-fi world. Go ahead, pick up the phone.
